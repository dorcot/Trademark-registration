How to Register a Trademark in Dubai

What is a Trademark?

A trademark is a symbol, word, design, or combination of these that distinguishes the goods or services of one company from others. In Dubai, trademarks are governed by the UAE Federal Law No. 37 of 1992, ensuring comprehensive legal protection.

Benefits of Registering a Trademark

  • Exclusive Rights: Grants ownership of the brand in the UAE.
  • Legal Protection: Protects against misuse or infringement.
  • Enhanced Brand Value: Adds credibility and attracts customers.
  • Asset Creation: A registered trademark becomes an intangible asset.

Steps to Register a Trademark in Dubai

1. Conduct a Trademark Search

Before applying, ensure your trademark is unique and not already registered. This avoids rejection and saves time.

2. Prepare the Necessary Documents

You’ll need the following documents:

  • Trademark application form.
  • Copy of the trade license.
  • A clear image of the trademark.
  • Power of attorney (if applicable).
  • Passport copy of the applicant.
  • Description of goods/services associated with the trademark.

3. Submit the Application

File your application online through the Ministry of Economy's website or via their service centers.

4. Pay the Fees

The application fee for trademark registration in Dubai varies based on the class of goods/services.

5. Examination of the Application

The Ministry of Economy reviews the application to ensure compliance with trademark laws.

6. Publication of the Trademark

If approved, the trademark is published in two local Arabic newspapers. This allows others to raise objections within 30 days.

7. Issuance of Trademark Certificate

If no objections arise, the Ministry of Economy issues a certificate of registration. This certificate is valid for 10 years, with an option to renew.

Cost of Trademark Registration in Dubai

The cost can vary but generally includes:

  • Filing fees.
  • Publication fees for newspapers.
  • Additional fees if using an agent or legal representative.

Common Challenges in Trademark Registration

  • Objections: Arising due to similarity with an existing trademark.
  • Incomplete Applications: Missing documents or incorrect details.
  • Legal Disputes: In case of opposition during the publication phase.

Why Choose Professional Assistance?

Trademark registration can be complex, especially for foreign investors unfamiliar with UAE laws. Professional agencies or legal firms can simplify the process by ensuring your application is error-free and addressing any objections efficiently.

Conclusion

Registering a trademark in Dubai is an essential investment for protecting your brand. By following the steps outlined above, businesses can secure their intellectual property and gain a competitive edge in the UAE market.
